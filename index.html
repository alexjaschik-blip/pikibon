01 About
A·J

A lens on
the human story

I am a filmmaker and videographer drawn to the places, people, and instants that most eyes pass over. My work moves between documentary and narrative — between the observed and the constructed — always searching for the image that holds its breath.

Born in the intersection of cultures, I have spent years cultivating a visual language that is spare, intentional, and grounded in the particular. Each frame is a decision. Each cut, an argument.

"Cinema is a mirror by which we often see ourselves."

02 Project

Doors
of Athens

An ongoing archive
of thresholds

Every door in Athens is a sentence. Some whisper of neoclassical grandeur; others scream in peeling paint and rust. All of them hold a world behind them — and a world in front.

Doors of Athens is an ongoing photographic and film archive documenting the entrances of one of the world's oldest inhabited cities. It is a meditation on thresholds: on what it means to enter, to leave, to stand at the boundary of two things.

What began as a private obsession has become a growing archive — a study in texture, colour, memory, and urban time. Each door is catalogued not only as an image but as a story waiting to be told.
